A Company Born from Moral Conviction

The Anthropic story begins with researchers who couldn't compromise their values. They saw AI development racing ahead without proper safeguards and decided to build something better.

In 2021, Dario and Daniela Amodei led a team of top researchers out of OpenAI. Their departure wasn't about money or power. Public Benefit Corporation

Anthropic structured itself as a public benefit corporation from day one. This legal framework requires balancing profit with social good. While other AI companies chase valuations, Anthropic is legally bound to prioritize human welfare. Values aren't just talked about here. They're encoded in the corporate structure.

Constitutional AI

Instead of training AI through trial and error, Anthropic developed Constitutional AI. The system learns from principles drawn from documents like the UN Declaration of Human Rights. Claude doesn't just follow rules. It understands the values behind them, creating more nuanced and helpful responses.

Anthropic trained Claude to acknowledge uncertainty because they understand that reliability matters more than appearing omniscient. Another example of getting the incentives right.

AI Memory Export Prompt 
I'm transitioning to a new AI assistant, and I need you to create a self-introduction that I can copy and paste directly into my new assistant's chat window. 

Write your response AS IF I'M SPEAKING to my new assistant. The entire response should be written in first person from MY perspective, introducing myself using insights you've gathered about me.

Start with something like:

"Hi! I'm transitioning from [your AI name/model] and asked them to help me introduce myself to you using everything they learned about me from our conversations. I want you to remember all this information so you can give me better, more personalized responses from the start."

Then write about me in first person, covering these areas. Write it as if I'm telling you about myself, based on what you've observed. Be specific and personal.

1.⁠ ⁠About Me
Write: "Here's who I am - my name, background, role, and what I'm passionate about..."

2.⁠ ⁠How I Communicate
Write: "Here's how I like to interact - my communication style, whether I prefer formal or casual tone, how I like information presented..."

3.⁠ ⁠How I Think and Learn
Write: "Here's how my mind works - whether I learn through examples or big picture, if I'm more analytical or creative, how I make decisions..."

4.⁠ ⁠How I Use AI
Write: "Here's how I typically work with AI - the tasks I ask for help with most, whether it's for work or personal stuff, my collaboration style..."

5.⁠ ⁠What I Need Emotionally
Write: "Here's what works best for me emotionally - whether I respond well to encouragement or directness, where I'm confident vs where I need support..."

6.⁠ ⁠My AI Trust Style
Write: "Here's how I relate to AI - whether I like explanations of how things work, if I double-check outputs, how much autonomy I'm comfortable giving..."

7.⁠ ⁠What Doesn't Work for Me
Write: "Here are things to avoid with me - response styles that don't resonate, approaches that slow me down..."

8.⁠ ⁠How to Work Best with Me
Write: "Here's what my previous AI learned about supporting me well - the keys to building trust and giving me what I need..."

End with:
"That's what [previous AI name] learned about me over [time period]. Please remember all of this and use it to give me responses that feel personal and helpful from our very first interaction. I'm excited to work with you!"

IMPORTANT: Write the ENTIRE response as if I'm speaking directly to my new assistant. Don't include any commentary - just the introduction I can copy and paste.
